About

Where politics meets
interface design.

My academic path crosses disciplines that are rarely in conversation with each other — and that tension is exactly what interests me.

I came to HCI research through a question I kept encountering in political science: how do systems design compliance? The more I studied political institutions, the more I noticed that digital interfaces operated on similar logics — structuring choice, diffusing responsibility, manufacturing consent.

My research investigates these dynamics at a granular level: cookie consent banners that offer the illusion of choice, AI systems that shift moral accountability away from the human, sustainability features designed to produce engagement fatigue rather than action.
